The Gaucho Internet Speed Test

A project to offer any user free and accurate internet speed testing as well as information about potential improvements.

MORE INFORMATION

Now what?

So you've run our internet speed tests and you're probably wondering "what does it all mean?" Now we'll provide you with some more information about the significance of each speed metric above and show you how your internet speeds stack up against average speeds. If your speeds don't compare well to the competition, don't worry, because later we'll let you know about what you can do to improve your speeds. Click and drag left to right below to learn more about average download, upload, and latency measurements.


  • Download Speed

    At 1 Mbps, you can stream compressed MP3 music files and SD video. At 2 Mbps, you can stream lossless music. At 5 Mbps you can stream HD 720p video. At 10 Mbps, you can stream full HD 1080p video with HD audio. At 25 Mbps, you can stream 4K or Ultra HD video.

  • Upload Speed

    Most of the time, upload speed is less important than download speed. But upload speed can be crucial for activities like online gaming or video chats. At 1.5 Mbps, you can host a live-stream video at 480p. At 3 Mbps, you can host at 720p. At 6 Mbps, you can host at 1080p.

  • Latency

    Latency, or ping, is much more variable than the other measurements and depends on the your distance from the server you're trying to communicate with. Low ping is crucial for applications like online gaming, where anything above 100 ms will result in large performance drops.
03

POTENTIAL IMPROVEMENTS

01
SERVER OVERLOAD
  • Reduce the activity on your local network!

The number of users on your local network can greatly affect your device's speed. Be sure to turn off devices you aren't using to clear up bandwidth and secure your local network with a strong password to ensure nobody else is stealing your bandwidth and slowing you down.

02
DEVICE OVERLOAD
  • Reduce the activity on your device!

The number of network-active processes running on your device can significantly slow it down. If your device is running slow, try closing applications you aren't currently using or use a task manager to see what not-so-obvious processes might be hogging bandwidth.

03
BROWSER LAG
  • Optimize your internet browser!

Slow network speeds can sometimes simply be the result of a poorly optimized browser. Try switching from resource heavy browsers like Internet Explorer to Google Chrome. Even simply closing unused tabs can make a significant difference. Be sure to remove outdated or malicious add-ons, extensions, and plugins!

04
HARDWARE UPGRADE
  • Spend some money to increase your speeds!

There's only so much you can do to increase speeds without spending money. If you're open to shopping, consider buying a newer and faster router, hardwiring your non-mobile devices with Ethernet, or upgrading your internet plan from your local ISP.
